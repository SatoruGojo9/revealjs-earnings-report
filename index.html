Risk Assessment Models

Value at Risk (VaR)

VaR at confidence level α:

\[VaR_\alpha = \mu + \sigma \cdot z_\alpha\]

Sharpe Ratio

Risk-adjusted return measurement:

\[S = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}\]

Where \(R_p\) = portfolio return, \(R_f\) = risk-free rate, \(\sigma_p\) = standard deviation

Capital Adequacy Ratio

\[CAR = \frac{Tier_1 + Tier_2}{RWA} \geq 0.08\]

RWA = Risk-Weighted Assets

These are the core risk models we use for portfolio management. VaR helps us understand potential losses at different confidence levels. Sharpe ratio shows our risk-adjusted performance - higher is better. CAR ensures we maintain regulatory capital requirements. Our current CAR is 12.3%, well above the 8% minimum.

Customer Analytics Query


-- Analyze customer transaction patterns
-- Query by: 24f1002697@ds.study.iitm.ac.in
SELECT 
    customer_segment,
    
    COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id) as total_customers,
    AVG(transaction_amount) as avg_transaction,
    
    SUM(CASE WHEN channel = 'mobile' 
        THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) as mobile_transactions,
    
    (SUM(transaction_amount) / 
     COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id)) as revenue_per_customer
     
FROM transactions
WHERE transaction_date >= '2024-10-01'
GROUP BY customer_segment
ORDER BY revenue_per_customer DESC;
This query helps us understand customer behavior by segment. We track total customers, average transaction size, and channel preferences. Mobile transactions are particularly important for digital strategy. Revenue per customer helps identify our most valuable segments.
